Why Road On

Owning property in Nigeria from abroad shouldn't feel like a second job.

Across the diaspora, the pattern is consistent. You send money for repairs that never get done. An informal caretaker — a relative, a neighbour, a trusted contact — lets things slide. You return to cracked walls, missing fittings, or a property that's been quietly occupied. For many homeowners, the cost of doing nothing is far higher than any membership fee.

"The problem isn't distance. It's the absence of a structured system designed for homeowners like us."

Road On was built to replace informal arrangements with professional care, give you verified places to stay when you visit, and make every trip home more affordable — through the power of a shared member network.

How It Works

One membership. Three things that change everything.

Road On brings together professional property care, a network of verified vacation homes, and an exchange system that makes visiting Nigeria dramatically more affordable — in a single annual subscription.

01

Professional Property Care

Enrol your property and a dedicated city manager takes over. Regular inspections, vetted maintenance teams, pooled repair reserves, and photo reports sent directly to you — so you always know your home is protected, not just promised.

02

Access the Vacation Network

Your membership unlocks access to a growing network of verified member properties across Nigeria. When you visit, stay in your own home — or exchange into another member's verified property in Lagos, Abuja, Port Harcourt, and beyond. No hotels. No uncertainty.

03

Travel Home for Less

Every stay in the network earns exchange points. Redeem them toward accommodation on future trips. With Lagos hotel rates exceeding ₦250,000 per night, members can recover their entire annual membership fee in a single visit home.
